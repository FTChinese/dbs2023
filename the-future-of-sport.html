The future of sport

What’s driving Saudi Arabia’s sports boom?

From the grassroots to the world stage, investment in sport is critical to the country’s economic diversification

Saudi Arabia’s victory over eventual champions Argentina in the first round of the 2022 World Cup highlighted the country’s sense of national pride and enthusiasm, and with international football superstars joining Saudi football clubs, fans had even more reason to celebrate as they welcomed their team home.

“Football has a large following in the region, but we had access to it only by going to an event internationally or watching it on television,” says Feras Sheraiff, Head of Sports, MENA Investments at PIF. “Making sure you have a local league with local heroes is very important.” Sporting heroes are indeed emerging across Saudi Arabia. 24-year-old psychology student and boxer Ziyad “Zizo” Almaayouf aims to become a world champion after six undefeated professional bouts, while 18-year-old Mashael Alayed became the country’s first Olympic swimmer at the Paris Games in 2024.

By inspiring others to lace up their gloves or head to the pool, these young athletes are critical to achieving Saudi Arabia’s Vision 2030 goal of having 40 per cent of the population engaged in physical exercise by 2030. A happy and healthy population is not only critical to Saudi Arabia’s long-term economic diversification: it is the driver for the country’s participation on the world stage.

A world of sporting success

Saudi Arabia is fast becoming a global sports hub, with PIF supporting the country’s aim of connecting to the global sporting infrastructure. Legendary European football clubs such as Real Madrid and Inter Milan have established partnerships across the country and, in June 2023, four Saudi football teams – Al-Ahli, Al-Hilal, Al-Ittihad and Al-Nassr – were transferred to companies owned by PIF and non-profit foundations, supporting their long-term growth and development through modern facilities, infrastructure and grassroots programmes.

PIF’s focus on sport also opens up the opportunity to build international partnerships. In August 2024, PIF struck a deal with Concacaf, the Confederation of North, Central America and Caribbean Association Football, that will increase the number of youth competitions across each region, boosting participation in sport.

Growing the game

PIF also sees grassroots sport initiatives as a way to empower people across society. The number of professional female football players in Saudi Arabia has increased 195 per cent since 2021: and at the local level, women’s participation in sports is just 2 per cent lower than men’s. To boost it further, PIF launched women’s sports and wellbeing company Kayanee in 2023, which offers Saudi women access to fitness classes, nutrition and learning resources online and in-person.

To become a cornerstone of women’s sport, PIF is focusing heavily on global events. In February 2024, it announced a partnership with the Association of Tennis Professionals and became the official naming partner of the PIF ATP Rankings. In May 2024, PIF entered a partnership with the Women’s Tennis Association (WTA) and became the first official naming partner of the PIF WTA Rankings. “Supporting the rankings fits really well with PIF’s ambition to support players through every stage of their tennis careers,” says Marina Storti, CEO of WTA Ventures, the organisation’s commercial arm, adding that spotlighting these stories and personalities is a way to engage people in issues outside of tennis.

“The partnership also demonstrates PIF’s long-term commitment to the wider growth of tennis,” explains Storti, pointing to the 2024 WTA Finals in Riyadh, where tennis legend Martina Hingis, PIF’s ambassador at the WTA Finals, and other WTA legends and players, conducted a number of tennis clinics, designed to share players’ knowledge with young people and boost grassroots engagement in the sport.

Saudi Arabia is home to 177 tennis clubs, up 146 per cent since 2019. In the last four years, the number of registered players has increased by 46 per cent to 2,300 and under-14 players by 100 per cent-from 500 to 1,000+.

Providing new opportunities for Saudi Arabia’s young population to connect and engage is essential to the country’s economic diversification. Backed by PIF, Savvy Games Group is driving this effort in the esports sector. This includes its acquisition of US games publisher Scopely, as well as developing state-of-the-art esports infrastructure such as gaming hubs and event venues. By hosting international tournaments and promoting gaming as a cultural and economic force, these efforts aim to establish Saudi Arabia as a global leader in the gaming industry while creating jobs, encouraging innovation and enhancing social interaction.

Electrifying sport

Sport is also a vehicle to drive change in other areas. Take PIF’s Electric 360 partnership, serving Formula E (auto) and E1 powerboat racing, which supports EV technology, as well as Extreme E offroad SUV racing – set to be rebranded as Extreme H – which is focused on hydrogen fuel.

“

This partnership is about a lot more than motorsport. It’s about developing new ways of mobility.

Alejandro Agag, chairman of Formula E, E1 and Extreme E

To win Formula E championships, competitors have developed batteries that can keep their car moving at up to 300km/h for 45 minutes without overheating or weighing the vehicle down. Each incremental improvement has implications for the wider automotive industry. “They test new things on the race cars and then they use them for the road cars,” explains Mr Agag. “That’s what gives Formula E its role as a laboratory for EV technology,”

SURJ Sports Investments, a PIF company specialising in sports investments, is pioneering advances in sports technology, transforming both professional performance and fan experiences. "Tech is a very big part of what we do," says Sheraiff. "When we look at SURJ, one of the areas they focus on is sports tech — addressing both professional and fan-level needs." With partners like Saudi Company for Artificial Intelligence (SCAI), SURJ leverages data to enhance athletes' performance and optimise team dynamics. Collaborations with PIF portfolio companies like SCAI ensure fans enjoy seamless experiences, whether attending games or engaging remotely. "It's about improving the journey — both for users of tech and participants — making sports more interactive and impactful," Sheraiff adds.

New era for sport

Over the longer term, PIF’s investment in sport will support the country’s broader economic diversification strategy. PIF-owned Sela, for example, has already brought to life hundreds of experiences across the sports and recreational sector, supporting a variety of innovative industries, while Qiddiya, expected to be the world’s largest entertainment city by 2030, is set to include an esports district, a 45,000-seat stadium and a Speed Park Track. Sports infrastructure will play a big role in meeting Saudi Arabia’s target of welcoming 150mn visitors per year by 2030.

With Saudi Arabia set to become one of the world’s top 10 tourist destinations before 2040, according to a Google study, sport is critical to keeping the region on the world stage. “We're bringing the region together,” says Mr Sheraiff. “Not only by finding our own national heroes, but celebrating the regional heroes as well.”
